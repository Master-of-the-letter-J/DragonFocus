Introduction
Dragon Focus App
The App to Help Concentration: Gives a Customizable Survey at the End of the day on how Concentrated the User has been and becomes angry or happy depending on the result, accumulating over days and weeks.


Check Out: Collaboration

Premise:


Summary
Everyday, you have 2 surveys: one to fill in goals to accomplish, and another in the evening of completing them. You can also check them throughout the day. By completing goals, you change the status of a ‘dragon’ — it’s happiness , health, and age.


* Function: To give motivation for achieving goals
   * Surveys: There’s a day and a night survey, which are both editable
   * Settings: You can make the Dragon More or Less Harsh on Reaction.
* Main Stats: Fury Scale (0 is no Fury/Maxed Yin/Min Yang, 100 is max Fury/Maxed Yang/Min Yin), and World Population.
* Monetization: A Subscription for More tools to make Scheduling, to Make Progression Faster, and to Buy In-Game Currency.
* General Layout: A top panel for all pages. A Main Page Displaying the Dragon, then a Lair Page with Logs, Achievements, A Graveyard, and Ascension, then a Market Page, then a Premium Page, then a Settings/Options Page (Survey and General Settings)


Competition
Finch (An AI Pet) and Habitica (Gamified Routine) Could Challenge this App. 


* Finch, Habitica        
* Unlike These Apps, Dragon Focus is designed to be more volatile in actions, which therefore can make completing tasks more urgent and more demanding.
* Dragon Focus is Also Designed for Simplicity, with a customizable task sheet that should be easy to use
* Dragon Focus utilizes streaks and its personality, alongside many notifications to keep users engaged.
* Overall, this makes for a simple, and extremely engaging app that attempts to have longevity, similarly to the engagement/surveys of Duolingo, with idle aspects similar to cookie clicker, and the main point being to-do and habit checklists similar to Finch, Habitica, or the Reminders app
